One of the basic operations with numbers is their comparison. We suspect that you are fluent in this operation and can compare any numbers, including integers. In this task, you need to compare two integers.

Everyone knows that "the sum does not change when the terms are rearranged."
However, it also happens that permuting two numbers leads to more interesting consequences. Let's say, for example, there are three numbers: a1, a2, a3. Let's consider the equality a1+ a2= a3. It may be false (for example, if a1= 1, a2= 4, a3= 3), but it may become true if you swap some numbers (for example, if you swap a2 and a3, it will become the equality 1 + 3 = 4). Your task is to determine, given the three numbers, whether they can be rearranged so that the sum of the first two is equal to the third.
